Gabriel is an Aristos, one of the elite of human society, a lord of technology. On the planet he built, in the Realized World, he is like a god.

"ARISTOI is a spectacular, large-scale space opera, in vivid color, with all the stops pulled out...a successful entertainment which leaves sobering questions to linger afterward." -- Locus

In the Oneirochronon, Gabriel is only one of many Aristoi, and not the greatest. There in the electronic world of the Hyperlogos, the Aristoi vie for power and prestige in ever-more-complex manipulations of their common virtual reality.

"ARISTOI is a highly colorful and inventive science fiction entertainment, fast-paced and meditative by turns." -- New York Newsday

Gabriel has discovered something that threatens the whole human race: somewhere in the galaxy is an Aristos who has gone mad.

"ARISTOI is an immensely ambitious tale, an opulent tour-de-force ... a masterful work." -- Washington Post Book World

Aristoi
Walter Jon Williams
1992, 449 pages

This is one of my favorite books. The idea of computer implantation appeals to me right at the outset -- but Williams presents an entirely new idea here, the idea of harnessing multiple personality disorder to improve the data throughput of the human mind! Wow!

And of course the whole idea of any meritocracy of technologically gifted individuals appeals to all geeks.

All that aside -- good book. One of the select few that I think really have something unique to say about the world.
