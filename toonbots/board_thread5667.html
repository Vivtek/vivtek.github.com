Oh. Sorry about this. You've hit on one of my favorite topics: How NYC Is NOT The Same As It Was In 1970.

NYC was a seething cesspool in the 70's, I'll freely admit. Garbage on the streets, prostitution all over, riots for the fun of it. Unfortunately, a lot of people visited in the 70's, went home and proceeded to tell the rest of the nation about it. Since then, we had a little thing called 'Mayor Guliardi (I can't spell his name)'. Mayor G cleaned up the city. He moved prostitution and drug dealing out of Times Square. He instituted regular garbage pickups. He poisoned all the darling little rat babies. He also created a fascist and totalitarian police force, but hey. Noone's perfect.

So after my little speech there...answers to your questions.

a) Depends largely on which show you want to see. There are half price tickets available on shows that aren't ragingly popular just now, just around the corner from me. These will generally run you around $60 a pop.

b) Is she planning on going to really bad areas of town? Because NYC is so heavily dependent on tourism, the tourist areas of town have a very impressive police presence. Now, if she heads out to the boroughs, to the bad areas out there...she might have a problem. Though, I worked in those bad areas for 8 months and never even felt threatened. I've lived here for 5 years now and haven't been mugged yet. Do bear in mind that in spite of the fact that it's our biggest city, NYC is not even in the top ten for crime in the nation. Stay in the tourist areas and you'll be fine.

Because I am an amazing host, if I'm not working I will of course drag you lot around the city and keep you out of trouble.
