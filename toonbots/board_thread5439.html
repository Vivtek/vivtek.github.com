But (1) Cuba is *big*, and (2) "right next" kind of skips Hispaniola, which is also a honking big island, big enough it needs two countries to cover it. Anyway, turns out I'm about as far from Miami here as I was in Indiana. And Miami is damned far from Indiana, as one discovers to one's chagrin when one attempts to drive it in any reasonable number of days.

I'm free associating again. I think I need to go to bed now.

Re: Guy with a bird on his head, $13.95 with deliv

> May I enquire as to whether the chap with the avian on his noggin is your
> own free-hand artistic rendering, or clip art from someplace else?

Neither. He's a Taino petroglyph -- the Taino were the indigenous people of the Caribbean and Florida when the Spanish came and killed most of them. They carved the coolest symbols on rock. The guy with a bird on his head is actually a very cleaned-up photo of a petroglyph. Nobody's 100% sure what the bird is supposed to mean, but the going theory is that it represents a hallucination (making it perfect for Toonbots.) The Taino drank nicotine stew until they vomited in order to hallucinate to tell the future. So the bird symbolizes wisdom, and that makes the guy with the bird on his head a shaman.

I've got more petroglyphs, including one that looks like a bunny rabbit. I'll be sprinkling them into the toon as time goes on. If I can get a picture of the sun monkey from Tibes (the Taino ceremonial ground here in Ponce) I'll use that, too.
