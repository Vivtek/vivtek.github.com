For the record, the differences between Keenspot and Keenspace include the fact that Keenspot was first, and the fact that almost anyone can put a strip up on the latter (when tech problems aren't overwhelming, anyway), while the spot strips are chosen. Does not mean that a given spot strip is inherently better than a given space strip. Still, there is a certain prestige factor involved, yes. Promotional advantages also exist, through more focused banner ads and newsboxes to promote a specific aspect of a strip. Keenspot has an e-mail subscription service for many of its strips as well (those whose authors agreed to it anyway), so hopefully the doodle will follow suit at some point. And though the success of banner ads and Keenspot Premium subscriptions have been debated, as far as I know, there usualy *is* a greater likelihood of receiving some sort of profit from one's strip on the spot than the space (again, as far as I've gathered.) Keenspot, though still known to have tech problems, is less likely to cause the major updating problems and other annoyances which seem to plague Keenspace cartoonists all too often.
