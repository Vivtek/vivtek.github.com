On film grading

I *do* grade high, just because I like movies a lot. So for future reference, here's what different numbers mean:

10 - surprised me with its quality; I don't think I could improve it. 9 - worth watching, even if there is the occasional mild flaw. Frankly, most movies fall into this category. 8 - something bothered me, either a specific problem or just the general feeling that the movie should have been better given the premise -- but I don't regret having seen it. 7 - the same as 8 except I wish I'd gone to the matinee. 6 - the same as 7 except I wish I'd waited for it to show at the mall for $1.50. 5 - distinctly bad. I don't regret seeing this if there was nothing better at the video store, and it wasn't a new arrival, and maybe it was just one of the 5 for $5.55 special or something. 4 - I was offended by this movie and what a waste of time it was 3 - Dom DeLuise was in this movie (nothing against Dom, I think he personally is pretty funny, but he really is a dead giveaway of a really, really bad movie.) 1 - Baby Geniuses.

Just so we're all straight on this categorization scheme and all.
