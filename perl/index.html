Perl
I first started getting back into Perl in about 2008, as far as I can tell. The reasons are somewhat murky, except for one: the CPAN. Thanks to the existence and ease of use of CPAN, the first thing I consider when attacking any new problem is Perl.

There's just one problem: due to historical reasons and the fact that the translation industry demands it, I work on Windows. Oh, I host servers under Unix, sure. But my personal machine is a Windows machine. Unfortunately, Perl and Windows are very often at loggerheads.

This little corner of the site will be devoted to articles about Perl, and because I use Perl under Windows, my focus will very often be Perl under Windows. This page in particular is more a placeholder for now; since I'm just now getting underway with my plan to write more articles, I don't have much to show. Yet.

So the first thing I want to address is UAC (User Account Control), which was introduced in 2008. I've put together a little module Win32::RunAsAdmin that bundles everything related to UAC into one convenient little package, and the article presenting that is the first Perl article I've written.

There will be more.
